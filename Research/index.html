Research

Artificial Intelligence and Data Science Research

Advisor: Professor Pavlos Protopapas / Institute for Applied Computational Science / Harvard University. Jul 2022 - Oct 2022.

  • Applied VGG-16 and AlexNet models to classify pictures of buildings damaged by hurricanes.
  • Used a transformation of continuous probability density function to address the singularity of the Dirac delta function as a point source.
  • Adjusted PINNs losses by adding a small positive term to the squared weights based on the uncertainty weighting method to make them converge to a lower level at a higher speed when solving partial differential equations.

Equivariant Neural Network Research

Advisor: Professor Renjun Xu / Data Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory / Zhejiang University. Oct 2021 - Dec 2021.

  • Applied equivariant neural network in predicting characteristics of energy band from the structure of crystalline substances.
  • Studied the combination of equivariant neural networks and attention mechanisms, capsule networks, etc.

Community Insight and Impact Research

Advisor: Professor Savannah Thais / Institute for Computational Science and Engineering / Princeton University. Oct 2022 - Present.

  • Summarized suitable community vulnerability metrics by considering epidemiological and healthcare system variables, socioeconomic status, household composition, disability, minority status, etc.
  • Used algorithms like CNN and Catboost to analyze the effect of restoration policies on public health, infrastructure, risk of economic harm, and so on.
  • Employed LSTM to analyze changes in American citizens’ sentiment towards the progress of vaccination based on tweets.

Machine Learning and Data Science Research

Advisor: Professor Mark Vogelsberger / Department of Physics / MIT. Oct 2020 - Dec 2020.

  • Used CNN to classify tumor images with GAN augmentation and built an NLP speech synthesis interface to communicate the diagnoses.
  • Applied LSTM and RNN in text translation and sentiment analysis of movie reviews.

Machine Learning and Data Stimulation Research

Advisor: Professor Thomas Sauerwald / Department of Computer Science / the University of Cambridge. Sep 2020 - Nov 2020.

  • Advanced resolution of tower images by combining SRGAN with Wasserstein GAN.
  • Used a network graph theory approach to increase the flexibility of airline networks.
